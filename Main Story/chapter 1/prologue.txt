# TOUHOU LABYRINTH TOWER — ISEKAI CAMPAIGN DESIGN DOCUMENT
## "Strangers from Beyond the Boundary"

---

## PREMISE

The Great Hakurei Barrier, already strained by the mysterious crystalline tower that materialized at Gensokyo's heart, finally cracked when the boundary-keeper Reimu Hakurei cleared the tower's 10th Floor. In that moment of spiritual resonance, rifts tore open between worlds — brief, unstable tears that pulled unsuspecting outsiders into Gensokyo. The rifts are one-way. The instability makes return passage impossible... for now.

The players are high school students from the modern world, dropped into the strangest land they've ever seen, with no way home — and a tower that might just hold the answers they need.

### Character Creation Note
Characters begin at Level 1 with modern-world starting equipment (phone with dying battery, school bag, whatever they had on them). Their abilities manifest as an instinctive response to Gensokyo's magic-saturated environment — Gensokyo *awakens* something latent in outsiders.

## THE FORGOTTEN ONE

**The third PC is subject to the Rift's Perception Distortion.**

*She is a cleric class
This character arrived through the most unstable tear in the barrier. The rift's energy warped how they are perceived — not invisibility, not silence, but a profound absence from the notice of others. People's eyes slide past them. Names slip from memory. They exist in a perpetual social blind spot.

### Mechanical Rules — Perception Distortion

**NPCs:**
- Any NPC who has not been formally introduced to The Forgotten One must make a DC 20 Wisdom saving throw to acknowledge them when they speak. On a failure, the NPC responds only to the other party members, as if no one else spoke.
- An NPC forgets The Forgotten One was present entirely after failing a DC 20 which happens every 6 seconds.
- Named, powerful NPCs (Keine, Sakuya, Reimu, etc.) make the save with advantage — their awareness is stronger.

**Party Members:**
- Once per long rest, each other party member must roll 1d20 at the DM's discretion. On a 20, they momentarily remember The Forgotten One (THE DM will give vague recollection on what happened so far with them)


**Mechanical Benefits:**
- The Forgotten One has Advantage on all Stealth checks.
- Hostile NPCs require a DC 20 Perception check to target The Forgotten One in combat.
- They can enter most locations without challenge — people simply don't register their presence as an intrusion.

**Narrative Weight:**
Other PC must be able to discern the presence of her

## LANDING LOCATIONS

When the rift deposits the players, roll 1d4 or let the DM choose based on the party's composition. Each location provides a different entry lens into Gensokyo's world.

---

### LANDING 1 — HUMAN VILLAGE
*For parties that benefit from a grounded, lore-heavy introduction*

**First Scene:** The players materialize in the middle of the village market in broad daylight. Villagers scatter. Stalls overturn. Within minutes, a firm but calm woman with white-streaked hair steps through the panicking crowd — Keine Kamishirasawa, half-beast history teacher, guardian of the Human Village.

**Keine's Reaction:** Controlled alarm. She recognizes the smell of a boundary breach immediately. She gathers the players quickly, gets them off the street, and sits them down in her schoolroom. She is not warm, exactly — but she is responsible. She will not let outsiders come to harm in *her* village.

**What Keine Teaches:**
- Gensokyo's nature: a sealed land kept separate from the outside world by the Great Hakurei Barrier
- The rules of survival: don't provoke youkai, don't wander at night, stay on known paths
- The tower: she explains it appeared weeks ago with an announcement from a girl in a shifting mask, and that since then, residents and outsiders alike have been attempting to climb it — some for fun, some for profit, some to understand what it *is*
- The Barrier crack: she reveals that when Reimu cleared the tower's 10th Floor, the barrier thinned dramatically — she suspects this is why the players arrived

**Lore Drop (Mid-Conversation):**
*"The announcement came through every part of Gensokyo simultaneously. 'Reimu Hakurei has reached Floor 10.' And then — the boundary weakened. For just a moment, I could feel the outside world pressing against ours. I suspect you felt that too, from the other side."*

**Companion Offer:** Keine offers to accompany them to the tower's base — not because she trusts them entirely, but because loose outsiders near her village is her problem to manage. She is protective, informative, and occasionally exasperated.

---

### LANDING 2 — YOUKAI MOUNTAIN
*For parties that benefit from an active, high-tension introduction*

**First Scene:** The players materialize on a rocky mountain slope surrounded by mist. Within two minutes they are surrounded by white wolf tengu patrols. Weapons drawn. Tense standoff. (There is a narrative option to flee and )

Then a cheerful, green-haired girl in shrine maiden clothes pushes through the wolves with her hands up — "I've got this! They're outsiders, not threats!"

Sanae Kochiya, wind priestess and relatively recent arrival to Gensokyo herself, is *delighted.*

**Sanae's Reaction:** Immediate excitement. Sanae came from the outside world herself, years ago, and she's been hoping to meet other outsiders ever since. She talks fast, references games and media the players might know, and is shockingly casual about how dangerous their situation actually is.

**What Sanae Explains:**
- She was an outsider once too — she understands the disorientation
- Youkai Mountain: home to the tengu (journalists and soldiers), kappa (engineers and inventors), and the Moriya Shrine Goddess faith
- She theorizes about the barrier crack openly: she was in the middle of a ritual when it happened, felt the fluctuation, immediately started scanning the mountain for anyone who came through
- The tower: she's been watching teams go in and out; she's intensely curious about it and frankly wants to climb it herself

**Lore Drop:**
*"When Reimu cleared the 10th Floor — oh, you probably don't know who Reimu is yet — there was this huge spiritual shockwave. Lady Kanako even spilled her tea. The boundary between Gensokyo and the outside? It just... flexed. Like a wall someone hit really hard. You must have been standing right against it."*

**Companion Offer:** Sanae joins them immediately, barely giving them a choice. She is enthusiastic, knowledgeable about modern-world references, and occasionally *too* confident about things she doesn't actually know yet.

---

### LANDING 3 — SCARLET DEVIL MANSION
*For parties that benefit from a high-stakes, socially treacherous introduction*

**First Scene:** The players materialize in the SDM's grand foyer. Tall ceilings. Red carpets. Hundreds of identical fairy maids who stop and stare. Within thirty seconds, a silver-haired woman appears from nowhere — standing directly in front of them, five throwing knives already between her fingers.

Sakuya Izayoi, head maid of the Scarlet Devil Mansion, appraises them with the cool efficiency of someone deciding whether to throw the knives.

**Sakuya's Reaction:** Measured. Professional. She doesn't act on the knives. She introduces herself as the mansion's chief maid and asks, with perfect politeness, what business outsiders have materializing in the foyer. This is not a conversation they can bluff their way out of — Sakuya has seen too many incidents.

**The Guest Exchange:** Mistress Remilia Scarlet is summoned (or rather, Sakuya informs the players they will be presented). Remilia is amused and theatrical. She declares them "honorary guests" — on the condition that they offer something of equal value. This could be information, a service, a skill demonstration, or simply acknowledging her nobility with appropriate reverence. Getting this right earns them safety; getting it wrong earns them a more complicated stay.

**What Sakuya Explains (as escort to the gate):**
- The mansion's rules: don't wander, don't touch things, don't provoke the residents
- Gensokyo's structure, briefly and efficiently — she has no patience for unnecessary explanation
- The barrier breach: she knew the moment it happened; her time-sense felt it; she explains Reimu's Floor 10 clear as the trigger
- The tower: she is evaluating it as a potential resource; Remilia is "considering" entering

**Lore Drop:**
*"When Miss Hakurei cleared the 10th Floor, time itself stuttered slightly in this region. I noticed. The Mistress noticed. Whatever that tower is doing to the boundary, it is not incidental — it is structural. You arrived because something in the tower wanted the boundary open."*

**Companion Offer:** Sakuya accompanies them to the tower base as a matter of the Mistress's interest. She is efficient, slightly intimidating, observant, and occasionally reveals surprising warmth beneath the professionalism.

---

### LANDING 4 — EIENTEI
*For parties that benefit from a mysterious, medically-flavored introduction*

**First Scene:** The players materialize in the middle of the Bamboo Forest of the Lost — except the bamboo parts before them, and a moon rabbit with long ears and a military-style uniform is already running toward them with a medical kit.

Reisen Udongein Inaba, earth-based agent of the Lunarian manor, was *already sent* to retrieve them. Someone at Eientei anticipated the arrival.

**Reisen's Reaction:** Efficient and slightly frantic. She checks them for injuries before saying much, then leads them through the bamboo with practiced surety. At Eientei, Princess Kaguya and the genius doctor Eirin Yagokoro receive them with gracious, layered hospitality — the kind that is also surveillance.

**Eirin's Revelation:** Eirin knew the barrier crack was coming. Lunarian science tracked the tower's structural interference with the Great Barrier weeks in advance. She anticipated outsider arrivals. The players are examined (medically, gently, without their consent being particularly asked), fed, and given clean clothes.

**What Reisen Explains:**
- The Bamboo Forest: dangerous to outsiders; they were lucky Eientei was watching
- Gensokyo's sealed nature and the Barrier
- The tower: Eientei has been studying it remotely; Eirin's assessment is that it's of scientific origin dressed in mystical aesthetics — which makes it more dangerous, not less
- The barrier breach: directly tied to Floor 10; Eirin has *data*

**Lore Drop (Eirin, directly):**
*"The tower has been weakening the Hakurei Barrier since it appeared — gradually, structurally. When Miss Hakurei reached the 10th Floor, she triggered a resonance point built into the tower's architecture. The boundary thinned for approximately forty seconds. You came through in that window. The question isn't how you arrived. The question is whether the tower did this intentionally."*

**Companion Offer:** Reisen accompanies them — Eirin assigns her, essentially. Reisen is capable, responsible, slightly prone to panic under pressure, and deeply knowledgeable about Gensokyo's hidden workings.

## THE TOWER BASE — BOUNDARY BAZAAR

*"By the time the players reach the tower, they will find it is not standing alone. The tower arrived and the world reorganized around it."*

The tower's arrival transformed a wide clearing at Gensokyo's heart into something unprecedented: a permanent, semi-organized economic zone. No one planned it. It simply happened — the way markets always happen when people want things and other people want to sell.

Tents. Stalls. Permanent structures going up week by week. The smell of cooking, magic, wood shavings, and money. Climbers coming and going through the tower's great entrance. Teams arguing over strategy. Spectators making bets.

The Companion who guides the players here will remark: *"It wasn't like this before the tower. It grew. Like the tower wanted witnesses."*

### TOWER BASE SHOPS & SERVICES

#### THE LANDING PAD — Ejection Recovery Station
**Operator:** A rotating pair of fairy medics (no names given; they change frequently)
When climbers are ejected from the tower, they land here — disoriented, stripped of floor-specific loot, recovered to 1 HP. The fairy medics provide basic first aid, a blanket, and about thirty seconds of sympathy before the next ejected climber arrives.

*Reaction to Isekai'd Players:* The fairies have seen everything by now. They hand out blankets without questions.

---

#### MYSTIA'S NIGHT LAMP — Food Stall
**Operator:** Mystia Lorelei, sparrow youkai
A grilled lamprey cart that operates from mid-morning through midnight. The food is genuinely excellent. Mystia sings while she works, which is either pleasant or slightly unnerving depending on the hour.

**Menu** (displayed on a charcoal-smudged board):
| Item | Effect | Price |
|---|---|---|
| Grilled Lamprey | Restores 1d6+2 HP | 3gp |
| Night-Eye Soup | Darkvision 60ft for 1 hour | 8gp |
| Stamina Skewer | Removes 1 level of exhaustion | 12gp |
| Mystery Special | Roll on Mystia's secret table | 5gp |

*Reaction to Isekai'd Players:* Curious. Asks questions while cooking. Will absolutely use her song to disorient a customer who tries to skip payment — "accidentally."

---

#### KOURINDOU ANNEX — Tools & Curiosities
**Operator:** Rinnosuke Morichika, half-youkai antique dealer
Rinnosuke opened an annex at the tower base when he realized climbers kept bringing out items he'd never seen. He identifies outside-world objects with his innate ability to know an item's name and purpose — though not always how to use them. He sells tools, utility equipment, and the occasional fascinating mystery item.

*Special Ability:* Can identify any non-magical item for free. Charges 10gp to explain its use.
*Reaction to Isekai'd Players:* Extremely interested. Modern-world students are walking inventories of outside-world objects. He will absolutely offer to buy the phone. He will offer too little for it.

---

#### THE WIND PRESS — Information Stall
**Operator:** Hatate Himekaidou, crow tengu photographer
Aya runs the Bunbunmaru Newspaper from the mountain — Hatate runs a competing gossip sheet at the tower base. She sells information, floor intelligence, and team tracking reports. Her cameras capture things people would rather she hadn't.

**Services:**
| Service | Details | Price |
|---|---|---|
| Floor Report | General info on current floor layout | 15gp |
| Team Intelligence | What a specific team is doing | 25gp |
| Rumor Package | 3 unverified rumors, mixed quality | 5gp |
| Photo Identification | Identify an unknown NPC from photo | 20gp |

*Reaction to Isekai'd Players:* Immediately suspicious they're a story. Takes their photo without asking. Offers them a deal: share information about the outside world for discounted services.

---

#### MEDICINE'S REMEDY CART — Antidotes & Alchemicals
**Operator:** Medicine Melancholy, doll youkai
A small cart covered in colorful bottles. Medicine has a genuine interest in poisons — and by extension, their cures. She sells antidotes, condition-clearing tinctures, and a few experimental compounds that she is eager for test subjects to try.

| Item | Effect | Price |
|---|---|---|
| Antitoxin | Advantage vs poison for 1 hour | 50gp |
| Clarity Tonic | Clears charmed/frightened | 35gp |
| Medicine's Special (Experimental) | Roll 1d6 — effects vary wildly | 20gp |

*Reaction to Isekai'd Players:* Clinically fascinated. Offers them a "free sample" of something experimental. They should probably say no.

---

#### THE BLUE DRUM — Weapon & Armor Outfitter
**Operator:** Raiko Horikawa, tsukumogami drummer
Raiko set up shop after noticing most climbers arrived poorly equipped. She doesn't make weapons herself — she *empowers* them. Her shop sells standard equipment but specializes in items she has rhythmically attuned, granting them a minor resonance with her storm magic.

**Sells:** Standard PHB weapons and armor at PHB prices. Attuned items carry a small enhancement (+1 to a single related check, not attack) for +25% cost.

*Reaction to Isekai'd Players:* Professional and fair. Asks what they need, sells them what they need. Tells them to come back when they have money. Respects people who know what they want.

---

#### YUKARI'S WINDOW — Boundary Consultation
**Operator:** A gap in the air behind an unmanned wooden counter with a sign that says "Leave Questions Here"
Periodically, a paper slides out from a boundary gap with written answers. Sometimes the answers are relevant to the question. Sometimes they are not. There is no fee listed. Payment is occasionally demanded retroactively, in forms that are never money.

*Reaction to Isekai'd Players:* The gap has already left a note for them. It reads: *"Welcome to Gensokyo. Don't die before the interesting part."*

---

#### AKASHIC ARCHIVE — Floor Research
**Operator:** Hieda no Akyuu, chronicler of Gensokyo
Akyuu recognized immediately that the tower was a historical event of the first order and set up a permanent research station at its base. She collects testimony from returning climbers, cross-references it with Gensokyo lore, and sells curated intelligence reports.

**Services:**
| Service | Cost | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| Floor Briefing | 30gp | General knowledge of current floor's theme and threats |
| Monster Report | 50gp | Advantage on first combat vs. researched creature type |
| Team Profile | 20gp | Background information on a specific team |
| Chronicle Entry | Free | Akyuu interviews you and records your account |

*Reaction to Isekai'd Players:* Immediately writes everything down. Asks pointed questions. Finds their arrival historically significant. Is not warm about it — she's professionally fascinated, not personally invested.

---

#### THE WANDERING CLINIC — Medical Services
**Operator:** Yagokoro Medical Annex (staffed by moon rabbit assistants; Eirin visits irregularly)
A clean, surprisingly well-equipped medical tent. Eirin doesn't run it personally — she sends assistants and checks in when she's curious about a case. Sells superior healing potions and offers treatment for floor-inflicted conditions.

**Note:** This is the tower base equivalent of Eirin's service — limited compared to her full clinic, but accessible without advancing through the hub.

*Reaction to Isekai'd Players:* Clinical. Professional. The assistants treat them as patients, not people. Eirin, if present, treats them as data.

---

#### PRISMRIVER STAGE — Entertainment & Morale
**Operator:** Lunasa, Merlin, and Lyrica Prismriver (with occasional Raiko appearances)
A raised wooden stage near the tower entrance where the Prismriver sisters perform for tips and the general atmosphere. Their music has a known effect on morale — climbers who listen before entering report feeling slightly more prepared, though this may be psychological.

**Mechanical Effect:** Listening to a full performance before entering the tower (15 minutes) grants one use of Bardic Inspiration that expires at session end.

*Reaction to Isekai'd Players:* The sisters perform regardless of audience. Lunasa may acknowledge the players with a slow nod. Merlin will absolutely play something directly at them.

## HOW TOWER BASE RESIDENTS REACT TO ISEKAI'D PLAYERS

**General Human Villagers passing through:** Curious, cautious. Outsiders aren't completely unheard of, but students in school uniforms are unusual. They'll talk, but won't trust.

**Youkai merchants/operators:** Treat them as customers first. What you can pay for, you can have. What you can't pay for, they won't give. No charity — but no hostility either, unless provoked.

**The Companion:** Speaks for them in early interactions, vouches for their non-threat status, and occasionally has to prevent them from accidentally offending someone.

**Established Climber Teams:** Sized them up immediately. They look weak. They'll be watched to see if that changes.

**The Tower Announcer (Hologram):** Does not appear at the base — only activates inside. However, on Day 1, when the players first approach the tower entrance, the announcer's hologram briefly flickers into existence outside — unprecedented. It looks directly at them. It says nothing. It disappears.

## FLOOR REVAMP — NEW CAMPAIGN FRAMEWORK

*All floors are rebuilt around the isekai premise. Returning teams from the existing campaign may appear as rival NPCs.*

---

### FLOOR 1 — "The Waking Dream"
**Theme:** Orientation / Tutorial
**Location:** Replica of the Human Village, slightly wrong
**Victory Condition:** Find and reunite 3 "Memory Fragments" — pieces of a shattered illusion construct that the floor's overseer is using to anchor its reality
**Time Limit:** None — the floor resets after 6 in-game hours if incomplete

**The Hook:** The floor looks like the Human Village. The players might even recognize parts of it from their landing. But things are subtly off — windows in the wrong places, shadows falling at the wrong angles, NPCs who repeat conversations in loops. This floor teaches observation and questioning reality.

**Key Mechanic — The Loop:**
Every 2 hours, minor elements reset: looted containers refill, defeated minions return. Players cannot rely on attrition — they have to find the fragments to break the cycle.

**Floor Boss:** The Wandering Doll — an autonomous Alice-construct gone rogue, dressed like a tiny human. It doesn't fight; it runs, hides, and manipulates the environment. Players must corner it, not overpower it.

**Isekai Moment:** The Forgotten One can be perceived normally on this floor — the floor's reality-warping nature actually works *against* the Rift effect, making them temporarily visible to everyone. A bittersweet taste of being seen.

**Completion Reward:** 300gp, 800 XP, Floor 2 access

---

### FLOOR 2 — "Root and Branch"
**Theme:** Survival / Resource Management
**Location:** Infinite Forest of Magic replica — dense, magical, alive
**Victory Condition:** Locate Marisa's abandoned research hut at the forest's center and retrieve the Starfall Compass before other teams
**Time Limit:** 90 minutes — the forest "breathes," shifting paths on a timer

**Key Mechanic — The Shift:**
Every 30 minutes, the path layout changes. Notes from previous visits become outdated. Teams that don't keep moving get disoriented.

**Key Mechanic — Magical Growth:**
Certain plants and fungi can be harvested. Used correctly, they provide buffs. Used incorrectly, they cause conditions. No labels. Research or experiment.

**Special Room:** The Mushroom Vault — a cellar beneath a massive toadstool containing the Starfall Compass, guarded by an Awakened Tree that speaks only in riddles.

**Team Twist:** Marisa herself appears mid-floor as an NPC, trying to retrieve her own compass. She's annoyed someone got here first. She can be negotiated with, tricked, or raced.

**Isekai Moment:** Modern-world knowledge of plants is irrelevant here — all species are magical. Players must admit they don't know and ask.

**Completion Reward:** 500gp, 1,000 XP, Floor 3 access

---

### FLOOR 3 — "The Crimson Threshold"
**Theme:** Horror / Survival with Shifting Alliances
**Location:** Misty Lake and Scarlet Devil Mansion replica
**Victory Condition:** Survive until dawn (60 minutes) OR destroy the Crimson Heart before then
**Time Limit:** None — but exhaustion accumulates in the cold mist every 30 minutes

**The Twist:** Dawn doesn't come naturally on this floor. Time is frozen at just-before-sunrise. To actually end the floor, someone has to reach the Clock Tower and destroy the Crimson Heart that keeps the clock frozen.

**Key Mechanic — The Hunter:**
A Crimson Wraith prowls the floor. Unlike standard damage, it can drain Will Points (a 3-point separate track per character). At 0 Will, a character becomes The Wraith's Thrall until freed. The Wraith cannot be permanently defeated until the Crimson Heart is destroyed.

**Key Mechanic — The Mansion's Guests:**
The SDM's upper floors contain information, resources, and locked rooms. But fairy maids patrol. The Wraith cannot enter — but the mansion has its own dangers.

**Isekai Moment:** The players may encounter a replica of themselves — echoes left by the rift that pulled them in, frozen in the moment they vanished. Looking at your own echo is a DC 13 Wisdom save vs. being Shaken for 1 hour.

**Completion Reward:** 750gp, 1,200 XP, Floor 4 access

---

### FLOOR 4 — "The Unmapped Wood"
**Theme:** Navigation / Information Warfare
**Location:** Bamboo Forest of the Lost
**Victory Condition:** Collect 4 Celestial Navigation Stones and complete the hidden path sequence (North→East→South→West) from the Northern Center

**Key Mechanic — Center Isolation:**
The forest splits teams into 4 isolated centers. No communication between centers. Teams don't know who is where.

**Key Mechanic — The Announcement:**
Every 10 minutes, a disembodied voice announces how many stones each team carries. This creates escalating aggression as the floor progresses.

**Key Mechanic — Riddle Notes:**
5 riddle notes hidden across the floor each provide +2 to navigation rolls. A fully prepared team with all 5 riddles needs only a 10+ to navigate successfully.

**Alternative Victory:** Celestial Anchor + Magical Rope combination, found in the Western Center's mirror pool and a specific event area respectively.

**Isekai Moment:** The forest's misdirection magic specifically targets *memory* — which means it interacts strangely with the Forgotten One. NPCs in the forest lose track of them even faster than usual. But the forest's own navigation constructs *can* see them, making the Forgotten One the only reliable guide through certain areas.

**Completion Reward:** 800gp, 1,458-2,000 XP (time-scaled), Ring of Protection (1st place), Floor 5 access

---

### FLOOR 5 — "The Descent"
**Theme:** Dungeon Crawl / Elemental Hazards
**Location:** Underground Hell of Blazing Fires (Former Hell replica)
**Victory Condition:** Reach the Palace of the Earth Spirits and present a "Proof of Passage" token to the overseer
**Time Limit:** None — but lava flow events trigger on a 45-minute cycle, cutting off paths permanently

**Key Mechanic — Heat & Darkness:**
Two environmental tracks: Heat (Constitution saves every 30 minutes vs. exhaustion) and Darkness (most darkvision is reduced by half; only magical light/devil's sight works fully).

**Key Mechanic — Lava Flows:**
At the 45-minute mark, 1d4 paths are permanently blocked by new lava flows. Teams that haven't mapped their route risk being cut off.

**Key Mechanic — The Earth Spirits:**
Minor earth spirits inhabit the floor. They can be befriended (giving route information and safe passage) or ignored (neutral) or harmed (actively hostile thereafter). They don't fight — but angry earth spirits can sabotage.

**Floor Boss:** The Furnace Golem — a construct of compressed volcanic stone that guards the Palace gate. It cannot be damaged by fire. Cold damage slows it significantly.

**Isekai Moment:** Heat has no mercy for modern-world students. This is where physical stats *actually matter* — cleverness alone won't save anyone from lava.

**Completion Reward:** 1,000gp, 1,600 XP, Floor 6 access

---

### FLOOR 6 — "The Storm's Eye"
**Theme:** Vertical Combat / Political Navigation
**Location:** Youkai Mountain replica — split into Tengu Territory (upper) and Kappa Territory (lower)
**Victory Condition:** Obtain sign-off from BOTH the Tengu Checkpoint and the Kappa Engineering Post, then present both stamps at the Summit Gate

**Key Mechanic — The Divide:**
The Tengu and Kappa factions on this floor are in cold conflict. Helping one faction in ways visible to the other costs you points with the other. True completion requires navigating both without alienating either.

**Key Mechanic — Elevation:**
Upper areas are accessible only via specific climbing routes or flight. Lower areas flood periodically. Teams that aren't thinking vertically will miss half the floor.

**Key Mechanic — The News:**
A tengu journalist NPC (minor character, not Aya) is following teams and publishing live reports. Actions become public. Reputation on this floor *spreads* to other teams in real time.

**Floor Boss:** The Summit Warden — a stone-and-wind construct that represents the mountain's own territorial instinct. It attacks anyone trying to pass the Summit Gate without both stamps.

**Isekai Moment:** The Forgotten One is *noticed* by the tengu journalist — their unusual perceptual status is a story. This is the first time an NPC pursues the Forgotten One specifically, for all the wrong reasons.

**Completion Reward:** 1,200gp, 1,800 XP, Floor 7 access

---

### FLOOR 7 — "The Mirror Shore"
**Theme:** Puzzle / Deduction
**Location:** A vast interior lake with a replica of the Misty Lake's shoreline — but the sky above reflects the world below and the world below reflects the sky
**Victory Condition:** Find the 7 Mirror Shards scattered across the lake's islands and reassemble the Mirror of Truth at the central island

**Key Mechanic — Reflections:**
The mirrored sky shows things as they were one hour ago. Players can observe "past positions" of other teams and puzzles by looking up. This is the only floor where information about the recent past is mechanically available.

**Key Mechanic — The Other Shore:**
Each island has a mirrored version slightly offset in the water. Some secrets exist only in the reflection — accessible only through specific magical interactions.

**Key Mechanic — The Ferryman:**
A ghostly ferryman transports between islands. He accepts memories as payment — recent ones. Each memory paid reduces that player's information about recent events (DM tracks what they've given up).

**Floor Boss:** The Mirror Doppelganger — a construct that takes the appearance of whichever character it last observed. It copies stats, abilities, and equipment. The only way to harm it is with attacks that can bypass reflection (radiant damage, damage to one's own reflection, or destroying the surface it's currently using as a mirror).

**Isekai Moment:** The reflections cannot show the Forgotten One. Where they stand, the reflected sky shows nothing. Their absence in the mirror is the most visible they've ever been.

**Completion Reward:** 1,400gp, 2,000 XP, Floor 8 access

---

### FLOOR 8 — "Bhava-Agra"
**Theme:** Endurance / High Stakes
**Location:** Heavenly realm replica — pristine white stone, peach gardens, clouds below, stars above
**Victory Condition:** Survive 3 Celestial Trials without being expelled from the floor

**Key Mechanic — The Trials:**
Three sequential trials, each themed around a virtue the floor tests. Players choose who faces each trial. Failure in a trial doesn't end the floor — but it marks that character with a Celestial Burden (disadvantage on one stat category) for the remainder.

Trial 1 — Trial of Endurance: A martial challenge that cannot be won by damage alone. The opponent regenerates. Only outlasting it ends the trial.
Trial 2 — Trial of Will: A psychological maze requiring the party to agree on a single answer to an unanswerable question. They have 10 minutes to discuss and reach unanimous consensus.
Trial 3 — Trial of Purpose: Each character must state, clearly and sincerely, why they are climbing the tower. The floor evaluates sincerity, not content — false or hollow answers trigger the Celestial Burden.

**Floor Boss:** Tenshi Hinanawi — present as a "floor guardian" who is mostly here because she finds the whole thing entertaining and picked this floor because the view is nice. She fights with genuine power but is susceptible to being *outplayed* socially.

**Isekai Moment:** The Trial of Purpose. This is the first time the players must say out loud, in-character, why they are climbing. For students trying to get home, this is the moment that crystallizes into genuine story.

**Completion Reward:** 1,600gp, 2,200 XP, Floor 9 access

---

### FLOOR 9 — "The Dreaming Gate"
**Theme:** Surreal Logic / Psychological
**Location:** Doremy Sweet's Dream Palace — a floor where the architecture follows dream-logic, not physics
**Victory Condition:** Find your own Dream Anchor (a personalized object tied to each character's deepest desire) and bring all three to the Palace Gate simultaneously

**Key Mechanic — Dream Physics:**
Gravity is variable. Doors lead to unexpected places. Time moves at inconsistent speeds. Some rooms require emotional states to unlock (being "hopeful," being "afraid," being "determined"). Investigation and Arcana checks determine how to interact with dream-logic rather than physical laws.

**Key Mechanic — The Sleepwalkers:**
Other teams on this floor are half-asleep — they function but their judgment is impaired. They might be allies, obstacles, or unintentional threats depending on what their dreams are doing to them.

**Key Mechanic — Personalized Anchors:**
Each character's Dream Anchor is different and reflects who they are. Finding yours requires engaging with the floor's dreamscape honestly — players who pursue the wrong object (what they *think* they want vs. what they *actually* want) find that the Anchor dissolves when touched.

**Floor Boss:** A Nightmare Construct — a shapeshifter that takes the form of whatever the current character fears most. It has no fixed stats. Instead, the DM sets its CR based on how specifically the player describes their character's fear.

**Isekai Moment:** The Forgotten One's Dream Anchor is *everyone remembering them.* The floor shows them a vision of being forgotten permanently — not just in Gensokyo, but everywhere. This is played gently. This is the floor where their arc becomes undeniable.

**Completion Reward:** 2,000gp, 2,500 XP, Floor 10 access

---

### FLOOR 10 — "The Boundary"
**Theme:** Story Climax / Revelation
**Location:** A fractal recreation of the Great Hakurei Barrier itself — walls of light, infinite corridors, impossible geometries
**Victory Condition:** Reach the center and speak with the Floor's Overseer

**This Is Not A Combat Floor.**

The 10th Floor does not have monsters. It has the answer to why the tower exists, who built it, and what the players are really here for. The Overseer — revealed here — is not an enemy. Whether they are a friend is something the players must determine.

**The Revelation:** Yumemi Okazaki, the tower's true architect, is waiting at the center. She is a scientist from the outside world who found a way to project a constructed space between dimensions. She built the tower because she needed people from many different worlds to test whether her rift-bridge could be made stable. The players were not accidents. They were *candidates.*

She offers them a choice:
- Help her stabilize the rift (multiple more floors to climb, possibly permanent residence in this space between worlds)
- Refuse and accept that the current rift remains unstable (they may find another way home, but it will be harder)
- Ask her something she hasn't anticipated (DM discretion)

**The Forgotten One's Moment:** Yumemi sees the Forgotten One clearly. Has always seen them. She apologizes — the rift that brought them through was the most unstable, and the perceptual distortion is a side effect of the crossing. She says she can fix it. She says she *owes* it to them.

Whether she gets the chance depends on the players' choice.

**Completion Reward:** 3,000gp, 3,000 XP, Campaign Milestone — and the knowledge that Floors 11 through 99 exist. Whatever the players decide, the tower is far from over.

## BARRIER THINNING — ONGOING CAMPAIGN MECHANIC

As the players clear floors, the barrier continues to thin. This is tracked as a narrative meter with mechanical effects:

| Floors Cleared | Barrier Status | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 1-3 | Strained | Occasional glimpses of the outside world in reflective surfaces |
| 4-6 | Cracked | New outsiders arrive in the village (introduce new NPCs or rival players) |
| 7-9 | Fractured | Players begin to receive fragments of memory from home |
| 10 | Critical | Yumemi's revelation unlocks; one-time chance of contact with home world |
| 11-20 | Volatile | The barrier situation becomes a Gensokyo-wide incident; major factions respond |
