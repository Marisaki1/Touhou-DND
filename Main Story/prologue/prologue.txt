# PROLOGUE — "An Ordinary Day (Until It Wasn't)"

---

## SCENE 1 — HOMEROOM

*It is an unremarkable Tuesday afternoon. The kind where the sky is too bright to look at and the classroom smells like chalk dust and someone's leftover lunch.*

Shuu Seiji is doing what Shuu Seiji does best — occupying space with maximum presence and minimum effort. He's at the back of the classroom, feet up, jacket draped over the chair like it owes him something.

Across the room — or nearby, or perhaps neither of you have noticed yet — there is IronAngel. Asleep. Specifically, asleep on top of a textbook that has not been opened today, yesterday, or arguably this semester. The pencil still loosely held in one hand suggests the sleep was recent and swift.

---

> **DM:** The classroom is half-empty before the final period starts. What are you doing right now, Shuu? IronAngel, you're awake — barely — what does waking up here look like for you?

*Let the players briefly establish themselves. One or two exchanges. Don't rush it — this is the last normal moment.*

---

## SCENE 2 — THE ANNOUNCEMENT

The school PA system crackles.

There's a brief feedback squeal, then the voice of one Chiyuri Kitashirakawa — third-year, science club, chronically overconfident in the practical applications of theoretical physics — comes through at a volume that is frankly unnecessary.

*"Attention, this is an important announcement from the Science Research Club! We have successfully opened a micro-scale spatial rift using the resonance equipment in Lab 3. We believe this constitutes a breakthrough in—"*

The PA cuts out.

There is approximately one second of silence.

Then, clearly audible through the intercom, the unmistakable voice of Yumemi Okazaki — student council president, two years running, the kind of person who has a contingency plan for her contingency plans — cuts in over the system.

*"This announcement is unauthorized. Everyone please disregard—"*

*"—the readings are off the charts, I'm telling you, the boundary coefficient is—"*

*"—Kitashirakawa, put down the microphone—"*

*"—it's already destabilizing, you can't just close the equipment now—"*

Then: a sound. Not loud, exactly. More like the air in the building changed pitch.

---

> **DM:** The PA goes dead. What do you do?

---

## SCENE 3 — THE QUAKE

It starts in the floor.

Not a violent shaking — more like the building took a single, slow breath and forgot to exhale. Desks shift. A stack of papers slides off a table. The fluorescent lights flicker once, twice.

Then it gets worse.

The windows rattle. Something on a shelf falls. The sound from outside — the normal sounds of a school in the afternoon, distant voices, the sports club, traffic — cuts off completely, replaced by a low hum that shouldn't be coming from anywhere.

---

> **DM:** This is real, and it is escalating. What do you do?

*Allow the players to act. Running into the hall, hiding under desks, heading toward Lab 3 — all valid. Track where they physically are when the next beat happens.*

*Note: whatever they are carrying on their person at this moment is what they bring with them.*

---

## SCENE 4 — THE RIFT

It opens in the floor.

Not in Lab 3. Not where Chiyuri's equipment is. Right here. In the middle of the ordinary classroom floor, between the rows of ordinary desks, a seam of light splits the linoleum like something on the other side is pushing through.

It is approximately one meter across and growing.

It looks like static. It looks like a hole in the sky. It looks like nothing that has a name in any language currently being taught at this school.

It is very bright. It is very loud — a high, resonant tone that you feel behind your back teeth. And it is pulling. Not physically, not yet, but the way a thought pulls at you right before you make a decision you can't take back.

---

> **DM:** It's right there. You have maybe ten seconds before it stabilizes or closes or does something else entirely. What do you do?

*If they jump in: they go through.*
*If they hesitate and get pulled: they go through.*
*If they try to run: the hum intensifies, the pull becomes physical, they go through.*

*They go through.*

## AFTER — LANDING

*They land wherever you've decided — Human Village, Youkai Mountain, Scarlet Devil Mansion, or Eientei.*

*They land together. Mostly.*

*One of them lands slightly apart. Or they all land together, but when they look around — really look — they might notice something that doesn't quite add up. A shadow that doesn't have anyone to cast it. A footprint in the dust that isn't theirs and isn't each other's.*

*Mari is there. She's always been there.*

*They just keep forgetting.*
